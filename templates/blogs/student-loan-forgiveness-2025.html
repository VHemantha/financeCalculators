02 Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

Public Service Loan Forgiveness remains the most powerful student debt relief program available. It cancels the entire remaining balance of your Direct Loans ‚Äî completely tax-free ‚Äî after you make 120 qualifying monthly payments while working full-time for an eligible employer.

PSLF Eligibility Requirements

  • 1
    Loan type: Must have federal Direct Loans. FFEL, Perkins, and private loans do not qualify unless consolidated into a Direct Consolidation Loan.
  • 2
    Employer: Must work full-time at a U.S. government organization (federal, state, local, or tribal), or a nonprofit tax-exempt under IRS Section 501(c)(3). New regulations effective July 2026 amend which organizations qualify.
  • 3
    Repayment plan: Must be enrolled in an eligible repayment plan ‚Äî currently Income-Based Repayment (IBR) or, from July 2026, the new Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP).
  • 4
    Payments: Must make 120 qualifying monthly payments ‚Äî 10 full years. Payments can be as low as $0 under IBR if your income qualifies.
‚ö° PSLF Buyback Program: If certain months of your payment history do not count because you were in deferment or forbearance, you may be able to "buy back" those months ‚Äî but only if you already have 120 months of qualifying employment and buying back would result in forgiveness.

Key 2025 PSLF Regulatory Changes

On October 30, 2025, the U.S. Department of Education published final PSLF regulations effective July 1, 2026 ‚Äî amending the definition of "qualifying employer" to exclude organizations with a substantial illegal purpose. These changes do not affect current borrowers or payment counts before that date.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important for Nonprofit Workers: Trump's Executive Order 14235 directed changes to PSLF employer eligibility. Some nonprofit organizations may lose qualifying status under new rules effective July 2026. Verify your employer's current status using the PSLF Employer Search Tool.

03 Teacher Loan Forgiveness (TLF)

Teacher Loan Forgiveness provides up to $17,500 in student loan cancellation for eligible teachers serving in low-income schools. It is a faster path than PSLF ‚Äî requiring only 5 years instead of 10 ‚Äî but the maximum forgiveness amount is lower and the eligibility rules are strict.

TLF Eligibility Requirements

  • 1
    Must be a full-time, highly qualified teacher ‚Äî meaning you hold a state teaching license not waived on an emergency, temporary, or provisional basis.
  • 2
    Must teach at a Title I school or educational service agency serving low-income families.
  • 3
    Must have taken out your federal loans before the end of your 5-year qualifying teaching period.
  • 4
    Critical: You cannot receive Teacher Loan Forgiveness and PSLF credit for the same 5-year period. If you have high debt and lower income, PSLF will almost always save you more money.
üèÜ TLF vs. PSLF Strategy: If your student debt exceeds $50,000, PSLF almost always wins mathematically ‚Äî full forgiveness after 10 years beats $17,500 after 5. Run the numbers using the FinWise calculator below before committing to either path.

04 Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) Forgiveness

Income-driven repayment forgiveness cancels your remaining student loan balance after 20 to 25 years of qualifying payments. It underwent its most dramatic overhaul in 2025 with the passage of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, which fundamentally restructured which plans are available.

05 Total & Permanent Disability (TPD) Discharge

Total and Permanent Disability discharge cancels the entire remaining balance of your federal student loans and TEACH Grant obligations if you are unable to work due to a severe, long-term disability.

  • 1
    Automatic discharge: Some borrowers are automatically identified as eligible by the Social Security Administration or the Department of Veterans Affairs and receive discharge without applying.
  • 2
    Physician documentation: If not automatically flagged, a licensed physician must certify a medically determinable condition expected to last 60 months or more that prevents substantial gainful activity.
  • 3
    Post-discharge monitoring: In most cases, you will be subject to a 3-year monitoring period during which income is monitored. Reinstatement can occur if income thresholds are exceeded.

06 Other Discharge Programs Worth Knowing

Beyond the major forgiveness programs, federal law provides several additional student loan discharge pathways that are overlooked by most borrowers:

  • üè´
    Closed School Discharge: If your school closes while you are enrolled or within 180 days of withdrawal, you may be eligible for 100% discharge of federal student loans taken for that program.
  • ‚öñÔ∏è
    Borrower Defense to Repayment: If your school misled you or engaged in misconduct, you can apply to have your Direct Loans discharged. This applies primarily to for-profit college situations.
  • üéñÔ∏è
    Military Service Benefits: The Department of Defense offers student loan repayment programs, active military service counts toward PSLF, and the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act caps interest at 6% during active duty.
  • üåø
    AmeriCorps / Peace Corps: National service through AmeriCorps counts toward PSLF. Peace Corps volunteers may receive partial cancellation of Perkins Loans.
  • üéì
    Perkins Loan Cancellation: Perkins Loans still in repayment have separate cancellation programs for teachers, nurses, and law enforcement officers ‚Äî up to 100% over 5 years.

07 The One Big Beautiful Bill Act: What Changed in 2025

On July 4, 2025, President Trump signed the One Big Beautiful Bill Act into law, making the most sweeping changes to federal student loan programs since the Higher Education Act. Here is exactly what changed and when:

  • Jul
    2025
    One Big Beautiful Bill Act Signed into Law

    Key provisions effective immediately: IBR no longer requires a "partial financial hardship" to enroll ‚Äî any borrower can access IBR regardless of income-to-debt ratio.

  • Aug
    2025
    Interest Returns to SAVE Forbearance

    The Department of Education began charging interest again on SAVE forbearance balances on August 1, 2025. Borrowers who remain in SAVE are now accruing interest on paused accounts.

  • Dec
    2025
    SAVE Plan Settlement ‚Äî Program Effectively Ended

    The Department of Education agreed to a settlement with Missouri effectively ending the SAVE plan. No new enrollments permitted; existing borrowers are being transitioned to other plans.

  • Jul
    2026
    Major New Rules Take Effect

    SAVE, PAYE, and ICR eliminated for new borrowers. Only IBR and the new Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) available. Parent PLUS loan holders must consolidate before this date to retain IDR access. New PSLF employer eligibility rules take effect.

  • Jul
    2028
    Old IDR Plans Fully Sunset

    PAYE, SAVE, and ICR access ends for existing borrowers. Everyone must transition to IBR or RAP by this date.

üö® Urgent for Parent PLUS Loan Holders: Borrowers with unconsolidated Parent PLUS Loans who want those loans to have access to an income-driven plan must consolidate before June 30, 2026, and enroll in an eligible IDR plan between July 4, 2025 and June 30, 2028. Missing this window could permanently eliminate IDR and PSLF access.

08 The SAVE Plan: What Happened & What You Should Do Now

The SAVE plan (Saving on a Valuable Education) was launched in 2023 as the most affordable income-driven repayment option. Its legal challenge, led by Missouri, resulted in the plan being placed in administrative forbearance ‚Äî and in December 2025, the Department of Education announced a settlement to end it, with its rules largely vacated.

If you are still enrolled in SAVE in 2026, you are accruing interest and earning zero progress toward PSLF or IDR forgiveness. Switch now.

What SAVE Borrowers Should Do Right Now

  • 1
    Switch to IBR immediately if you have 20‚Äì25 years of qualifying IDR payments and are close to cancellation eligibility. This is time-sensitive ‚Äî staying in SAVE forfeits progress toward forgiveness.
  • 2
    If pursuing PSLF: Time in SAVE forbearance can count toward PSLF through the PSLF Buyback program, but only if you already have 120 months of qualifying employment. Otherwise, switch to IBR now to resume PSLF-eligible payments.
  • 3
    Do not wait for a rescue. The Department will not forgive any loans through the SAVE Plan and is actively moving borrowers into alternative plans.
  • 4
    Apply through StudentAid.gov ‚Äî over 700,000 IDR applications were pending as of December 2025. Use the web application with IRS data-sharing consent for the fastest processing.

11 Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for student loan forgiveness?
Eligibility depends on the program. PSLF requires 120 qualifying payments at a government or 501(c)(3) nonprofit employer. Teacher Loan Forgiveness requires 5 years full-time at a low-income school. IDR forgiveness applies after 20‚Äì25 years of income-driven payments. TPD discharge applies to permanently disabled borrowers with qualifying documentation. Private loans do not qualify for any federal forgiveness program.
Is student loan forgiveness taxable in 2025 and 2026?
PSLF and Teacher Loan Forgiveness are permanently tax-free at the federal level. IDR forgiveness was tax-free through 2025 under the American Rescue Plan Act, but that exemption expired. Borrowers receiving IDR cancellation in 2026 or later may owe federal income tax on the forgiven amount ‚Äî the so-called "tax bomb." Plan accordingly by saving separately for this potential liability.
What is the SAVE plan and is it still available?
The SAVE plan was the Biden administration's most affordable IDR option, but it was blocked by courts after a legal challenge led by Missouri. A December 2025 settlement effectively ended the plan. New enrollments are no longer accepted. Existing borrowers are being transitioned to IBR or other plans, and interest has been accruing on SAVE balances since August 1, 2025. Time in SAVE forbearance does not count toward IDR forgiveness timelines.
Can I get both Teacher Loan Forgiveness and PSLF?
Technically yes, but not for the same service period. You cannot use the same 5 years of teaching to qualify for both programs. However, you could complete Teacher Loan Forgiveness after 5 years and then continue working toward PSLF, reaching full forgiveness after 10 more years. For most teachers with large debts, PSLF is the better financial choice because the entire remaining balance is forgiven rather than just $17,500.
Do private student loans qualify for forgiveness?
No. All federal forgiveness programs ‚Äî PSLF, Teacher Loan Forgiveness, IDR forgiveness, and TPD discharge ‚Äî apply only to federal student loans. Private loans from banks, credit unions, or private lenders are not eligible. If you are struggling with private loans, refinancing, direct negotiation with your lender, or lender-specific hardship forbearance are your main options.
What is the new Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)?
The RAP is a new income-driven repayment plan created by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, launching no later than July 1, 2026. It replaces SAVE and PAYE for new borrowers. Payments under RAP count toward PSLF. The plan generally sets payments at a percentage of discretionary income but is considered more expensive for most borrowers than SAVE was. Existing borrowers can access IBR through June 2028 before being required to transition.

What Should You Do Right Now?

The landscape for student loan forgiveness in 2025‚Äì26 is more complex than at any prior point ‚Äî but the core logic is unchanged: federal forgiveness programs are real, valuable, and still available for borrowers who qualify and plan strategically.

Here is the distilled action plan regardless of your situation:

  • 1
    Know what loans you have. Log in to studentaid.gov and confirm whether your loans are Direct Loans ‚Äî the prerequisite for PSLF and most IDR plans.
  • 2
    Identify your employer category. Government or 501(c)(3)? You likely qualify for PSLF. Low-income school teacher? Consider Teacher Loan Forgiveness. Private sector? Focus on IDR or aggressive payoff.
  • 3
    Get off SAVE immediately if you are still enrolled. Switch to IBR using your StudentAid.gov account. Time in SAVE forbearance does not build toward forgiveness.
  • 4
    Parent PLUS holders ‚Äî act before June 30, 2026. Consolidate and enroll in an IDR plan now or permanently lose IDR and PSLF eligibility.
  • 5
    Run your numbers. Use the FinWise Student Loan Calculator to compare PSLF, aggressive payoff, and refinancing with your actual balance and income.
  • 6
    Plan for the tax bill. If IDR forgiveness is in your future, build a dedicated savings fund for the potential federal tax liability ‚Äî start now, even if forgiveness is 15 years away.
